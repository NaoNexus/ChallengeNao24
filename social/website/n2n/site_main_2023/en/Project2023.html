Our project for the NAO Challenge 2023 revolves around the theme of eco-living. We chose this option because we firmly believe in its actuality, considering the hot topic of climate change. This year we are supported by Amperia, an important company in the sector of energetic eco-sustainability with ramifications throughout Italy. Their main objective is to increase the efficiency and reduce the emissions of all types of structures through improvements to their electrical systems. Their expertise has allowed us to widen our reach, with the goal of achieving new feats in a technological field destined to grow exponentially in the future.

For this NaoChallenge, the NaoNexus Team developed an interactive system aimed at analysing to the finest detail the energy consumption of a client and, subsequently, propose a renewable energy transition plan to convert a standard household into a completely energy-indipendent one.
Moreover, the NAO robot is also programmed and thought to be used as a domotic controller, like Alexa or Google Home, but with more features, more customization and a more user friendly way to interact than traditional controllers.

NAO is integrated with SolarEdge via selenium

Solaredge explained by Amperia's CEO

NAO is at the fulcrum of the process: with the help of the SolarEdge platform, our friend uses the retrieved data to offer smart suggestions aimed at improving the efficiency of the studied household, offering different renewable energy transition options for residential or commercial structures, with every type of budget fragment. Thanks to SolarEdge's proprietary algorithms, NAO can also provide information, based on how much CO2 the custom-generated solution offsets, on how many trees would be needed to plant to achieve the same result.

NAO's real-time analysis of carbon dioxide levels, air quality, temperature, humidity and other parameters, is then communicated to a potential user in the form of a dialogue with the robot itself. Based on this data, the robot can make automatic decisions and suggest solutions to make the best out of the climate control system of the building.
In public buildings, NAO can also be used as hub to manage temperature room profiles, automatic on and off switching of certain applicances, and readily available and user friendly domotic controller.

Meeting with Amperia

On March 24th 2023, the NaoNexus team traveled to Amperia's offices to discuss on the project. They were welcomed by Massimo Polato, CEO of the company. He helped us refine our work and provided relevant insights on clean energy production and consumption.

The key points discussed were experimentation, data collection and raising awareness. The NaoNexus team and Amperia agree that, while the first two are undoubtedly important, especially on technical side of things, they are subordinate to the third. We both believe that in order to fix a problem, one must first understand it. Being familiar with one’s individual carbon footprint is necessary to find suitable solutions intended to reduce it. From solar communities to new technologies aimed at producing and storing clean energy, the team left the meeting a new, enriched perspective.
